## Cross-generation invariants

### 1. Repositioning is structural

**COMPUTED.** Every generation shows at least 22.88 voluntary exits per 100 turns, and 49 of 50 games contain a hard switch. The rate is not confined to one ruleset: Gen 6 is 22.88, Gen 7 is 36.12, Gen 8 is 32.96, and Gen 9 is 23.40 per 100 turns.

**JUDGED.** Across generations, Empo repeatedly treats the active slot as temporary and routes through multiple pieces to preserve matchups or manufacture the next favorable exchange. This appears in an extended Gen 6 defensive route, a Gen 7 U-turn chain, early Gen 8 double-switching, and the staged Gen 9 answer to Dragonite. (`smogtours-gen6ou-627181`, T4–T18; `smogtours-gen7ou-772011`, T14–T19; `smogtours-gen8ou-470762`, T2–T7; `smogtours-gen9ou-790679`, T18–T24)

### 2. Setup is punctuation, not the base language

**COMPUTED.** Dedicated setup remains below 5% of Empo’s selected moves in every generation: 4.19% in Gen 6, 2.16% in Gen 7, 4.44% in Gen 8, and 4.48% in Gen 9.

**JUDGED.** The recurring sequence is to establish position, status, item or hazard progress first and deploy setup only when it converts a specific board. A 140-turn Gen 6 game contains one setup selection at turn 22; the long Gen 7 game separates its early and late Calm Minds by 90 turns; a 144-turn Gen 8 win never sets up; and the Gen 9 comeback waits until turns 20 and 22. (`smogtours-gen6ou-627181`, T1–T22; `smogtours-gen7ou-627209`, T21, T111, T120; `smogtours-gen8ou-551299`, T1–T144; `smogtours-gen9ou-772080`, T20–T22)

### 3. A one-piece deficit does not force an immediate all-in

**COMPUTED.** Empo wins 19 of 23 games in which his maximum deficit is exactly one Pokémon, with at least one such recovery in each represented generation.

**JUDGED.** The recovery mechanism varies—Web-enabled offense, a setup-and-removal sequence, measured trading around Heatran and Kartana, or status into a Skarmory close—but the invariant is behavioral: after falling one piece behind, Empo often continues to sequence through multiple turns rather than converting immediately into a single irreversible gamble. (`smogtours-gen6ou-532032`, T3–T10; `smogtours-gen7ou-626209`, T11–T25; `smogtours-gen8ou-530161`, T18–T38; `smogtours-gen9ou-772080`, T19–T26)

### 4. Opening role is adapted to the structure

**COMPUTED.** Normalized lead entropy exceeds 0.91 in all four generations, with 25 lead species overall.

**JUDGED.** Across the corpus, the first active Pokémon is selected for the immediate task: hazard establishment, Taunt and item disruption, status, direct pressure, or a neutral pivot. The same player-level pattern persists while the actual species and action vary by generation. (`smogtours-gen6ou-531323`, T1; `smogtours-gen7ou-772011`, T1–T5; `smogtours-gen8ou-470762`, T1–T6; `smogtours-gen9ou-790679`, T1–T5)

### What does *not* qualify as an invariant

**COMPUTED.** Hazard use does not clear the cross-generation bar: Gen 6–8 show a hazard-setting selection in 80.0%–88.0% of games, but Gen 9 shows only two selections in four games, on turns 16 and 17. The Gen 9 sample is also only four games.

**JUDGED.** The corpus supports “hazard-centric in Generations 6–8” more strongly than “hazard-centric regardless of generation.” Calling it universal would overstate the evidence. (`smogtours-gen9ou-790679`, T16; `smogtours-gen9ou-773677`, T17)

## Generation-specific technique

### Gen 6 OU: polarized structures

**COMPUTED.** The six Gen 6 games have a median of 21 turns but a range of 9–140, the widest proportional spread of any generation in the corpus.

**JUDGED.** The small Gen 6 sample splits between highly compressed Web offense and patient resource attrition. In one nine-turn game, Sticky Web on turn 1 and Nasty Plot on turn 4 produce a Thundurus-Therian close; in another, Web and Stealth Rock on turns 1–2 precede two Tail Glows and a turn-10 finish. The 140-turn game instead repeatedly resets hazards, applies Spore, recovers, and survives through a long final Slowbro/Clefable/Tornadus sequence. No single Gen 6 label covers all three. (`smogtours-gen6ou-531323`, T1–T9; `smogtours-gen6ou-532032`, T1–T10; `smogtours-gen6ou-627181`, T9–T140)

### Gen 7 OU: pivot-dense control

**COMPUTED.** Gen 7 has the highest pivot-exit rate, 7.64 per 100 turns, and the highest pivot-move share, 11.85% of Empo’s moves. It also contains 42 hazard-setting selections and 11 removal selections.

**JUDGED.** The distinctive technique is repeated initiative recycling: Tornadus-Therian and Landorus-Therian chain U-turns before hazards are reset, while longer games repeatedly alternate hazards, removal, recovery, and matchup changes. The result is not constant attack; it is control through successive active-slot advantages. (`smogtours-gen7ou-772011`, T14–T22 and T34–T58; `smogtours-gen7ou-627209`, T2–T10, T24–T52, T80–T93)

### Gen 8 OU: the deepest accumulation games

**COMPUTED.** Gen 8 has the longest median game at 41 turns, the highest mean at 56.44, the highest hazard-setting share at 10.78%, and 102 of the corpus’s 159 hazard-setting selections.

**JUDGED.** The Gen 8 sample most strongly expresses repeated accumulation: re-laying Stealth Rock after Defog, forcing item and status attrition, and preserving enough pieces to continue the loop. The successful version appears in the 144-turn final and the 101-turn hazard-stack win; the failed version appears in the 191-turn loss, where repeated Bulk Up and late switching no longer improve the terminal matchup. (`smogtours-gen8ou-551299`, T1–T144; `smogtours-gen8ou-478213`, T15–T101; `smogtours-gen8ou-470762`, T72–T191)

### Gen 9 OU: late resource compression

**COMPUTED.** The four attributable Gen 9 games last 20, 24, 24, and 26 turns. Empo makes only two hazard-setting selections across them and Terastallizes on turns 17, 20, and 24 in three games.

**JUDGED.** The visible Gen 9 technique is compressed endgame resource management: status or chip establishes a narrow closing board, then Tera is either unused or appears at the conversion point. This is a strong description of these four games, not a stable population claim about Empo’s Gen 9 play. (`smogtours-gen9ou-770676`, T17–T20; `smogtours-gen9ou-772080`, T9–T26; `smogtours-gen9ou-773677`, T17–T24; `smogtours-gen9ou-790679`, T19–T24)

## Style profile on six axes

### 1. Risk pricing: variance-seeking ← **moderately variance-suppressing** → suppressing

**JUDGED.** Placement: roughly two-thirds toward variance suppression, but not at the extreme. Empo frequently buys incremental control before exposing a win condition: Stealth Rock, repeated paralysis, Spikes, and Toxic are layered across turns 2–20 in one win; a 126-turn game keeps renewing hazards before late Calm Mind conversion; and a 144-turn win repeatedly resets Stealth Rock without any Empo setup move. The counterweight is that he will choose a compressed high-variance line when the structure supports it, as in turn-1 Web into turn-4 Nasty Plot and a nine-turn finish. (`smogtours-gen8ou-532021`, T2–T20; `smogtours-gen7ou-627209`, T2–T120; `smogtours-gen8ou-551299`, T2–T123; `smogtours-gen6ou-531323`, T1–T9)

### 2. Tempo: initiative-forcing ← **balanced, slightly counter-punching** → counter-punching

**JUDGED.** Placement: near the center, with a slight counter-punching lean. Empo can seize tempo immediately—Tornadus-Therian uses Taunt twice, Knock Off, and Hurricane in the first five turns, and Shuckle/Web offense commits by turn 4. But several signature sequences allow the opponent to reveal or advance a line before Empo routes to the answer: Dragonite receives multiple Dragon Dances before Thunder Wave, Volt Switch, sacrifice, and Tera Ghost Kingambit; an opposing turn-9 Tera is met by status and delayed Skarmory conversion; and in the 191-turn loss, Empo repeatedly responds to the opponent’s switching and recovery rather than forcing a clean terminal break. (`smogtours-gen7ou-772011`, T1–T5; `smogtours-gen6ou-531323`, T1–T4; `smogtours-gen9ou-790679`, T19–T24; `smogtours-gen9ou-772080`, T9–T26; `smogtours-gen8ou-470762`, T149–T191)

### 3. Progress mode: tactical burst ← **strongly accumulation-oriented** → grind/accumulation

**JUDGED.** Placement: about three-quarters toward accumulation. The clearest wins are built by repeating small irreversible gains: hazards are renewed through turn 93 before a late Reuniclus sequence; Stealth Rock is repeatedly restored through turn 123 before the 144-turn finish; and a Gen 6 game combines hazards, Spore, recovery, and Future Sight over 140 turns. The nine-turn Web/Nasty Plot win proves that tactical burst is in range, but it is the exception against the corpus’s larger mass of switching, hazards, status, and recovery. (`smogtours-gen7ou-627209`, T2–T126; `smogtours-gen8ou-551299`, T2–T144; `smogtours-gen6ou-627181`, T2–T140; `smogtours-gen6ou-531323`, T1–T9)

### 4. Information handling: early-committing ← **scout-patient** → maximally patient

**JUDGED.** Placement: clearly scout-patient. The three observed Empo Teras are held until turns 17, 20, and 24, while a fourth game ends without using Tera after the opponent commits on turn 9. Earlier-generation examples show the same behavior without Tera: Tornadus-Therian opens by denying and removing information-bearing items before the broader pivot cycle, and long games delay decisive setup until the board has been repeatedly tested. This is observable commitment timing, not a claim about hidden thought process. (`smogtours-gen9ou-770676`, T17; `smogtours-gen9ou-773677`, T20; `smogtours-gen9ou-790679`, T24; `smogtours-gen9ou-772080`, T9–T26; `smogtours-gen7ou-772011`, T1–T19; `smogtours-gen7ou-627209`, T21, T111, T120)

### 5. Preparation weight: standard executor ← **preparation-varied within corpus** → novel teams/sets

**JUDGED.** Placement: modestly toward the preparation-weighted end on a corpus-internal proxy, not on literal metagame novelty. Forty-three exact team compositions in 50 games show lineup variation, and the executed plans range from dedicated Web offense, to a Clefable/Heatran/Landorus long-control sequence, to a Samurott-Hisui/Kingambit short close. Yet the logs do not reveal usage baselines, authorship, or whether any set was standard at the relevant time. The defensible judgment is “varied preparation and structure selection,” not “innovator.” (`smogtours-gen6ou-531323`, T1–T9; `smogtours-gen8ou-551299`, T1–T144; `smogtours-gen9ou-790679`, T1–T24)

### 6. Range: specialist ← **universalist within OU** → universalist

**JUDGED.** Placement: strongly toward universalist within the boundaries of OU, but unscored beyond OU because the corpus contains no other tier. Empo wins with nine-turn Web offense in Gen 6, a 120-turn pivot-control game in Gen 7, a 144-turn attrition game in Gen 8, and a 26-turn no-Tera comeback in Gen 9. That is broad structural and generational range inside one format family. It does not establish universality across lower tiers, doubles, or other competitive environments. (`smogtours-gen6ou-531323`, T1–T9; `smogtours-gen7ou-772011`, T1–T120; `smogtours-gen8ou-551299`, T1–T144; `smogtours-gen9ou-772080`, T9–T26)

## Open observations

### Losses become more active, not less active

**COMPUTED.** Losses contain more voluntary exits than wins: 37.17 versus 30.95 per 100 turns. They also contain more pivot exits, 7.33 versus 4.06 per 100 turns, and a higher raw dedicated-setup share, 7.71% versus 2.55%. Even after removing the two long setup-heavy losses, the remaining-loss setup share is 5.08%, still above the win share.

**JUDGED.** When a position deteriorates, Empo’s observable response is often to increase the frequency of attempts to alter it—more switching, more pivoting, or repeated setup—rather than simply shortening the game. The 191-turn loss cycles Bulk Up into Haze and later through a long switch sequence; the 115-turn loss returns to Iron Defense on turns 68, 72, 82, and 113 before being broken; and the Gen 7 loss uses 11 pivot exits before a three-Pokémon deficit becomes final. (`smogtours-gen8ou-470762`, T50–T87 and T175–T191; `smogtours-gen8ou-772055`, T68–T82 and T108–T115; `smogtours-gen7ou-542402`, T1–T13 and T42–T49)

### Hazard persistence matters more than hazard speed

**COMPUTED.** The first hazard-setting selection is early—median turn 3—but there are 159 total selections across only 41 hazard-using games, an average of 3.88 selections in those games. The distribution is highly concentrated in long games: `smogtours-gen7ou-627209` contains 16 and `smogtours-gen8ou-551299` contains 12.

**JUDGED.** The more distinctive habit is not merely “sets hazards early”; it is “re-buys hazards when removal or board turnover makes them valuable again.” That distinction explains why the same nominal move appears deep into turns 80–123 and why hazard pressure remains part of the endgame architecture rather than only the opening. (`smogtours-gen7ou-627209`, T46–T93; `smogtours-gen8ou-551299`, T103–T123; `smogtours-gen6ou-627181`, T31–T66)

### Tera is used as a bridge as often as a finisher

**JUDGED.** Two of the three Empo Teras do not simply create an immediate sweep. Kingambit’s turn-20 Ghost Tera survives at 1%, damages Ogerpon, and dies two turns later, but that bridge leaves Landorus plus the existing Stealth Rock to remove the final three opposing pieces. In the other Kingambit game, Tera directly blanks the final Dragonite attack. Iron Valiant is the pure finisher case, taking the last three KOs after its turn-17 Tera. The commonality is terminal-board conversion; the exact role can be survivor, shield, or sweeper. (`smogtours-gen9ou-773677`, T20–T24; `smogtours-gen9ou-790679`, T23–T24; `smogtours-gen9ou-770676`, T17–T20)

### The profile’s central tension is breadth of structure versus narrowness of evidence

**COMPUTED.** The corpus spans four generations, 43 exact team compositions, 25 leads, and game lengths from 9 to 191 turns, but it contains only OU and only 50 attributable games, with 25 of them in Gen 8 and only 4 in Gen 9.

**JUDGED.** The strongest conclusion is not that Empo has one fixed archetypal style. It is that several decision habits—repositioning, delayed commitment, one-piece-deficit resilience, and setup as a selective converter—persist while the surrounding team structure changes radically. Claims about cross-tier universality, metagame innovation, exact historical development, or current form are not determinable from this corpus.
